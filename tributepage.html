MOHANDAS KARAMCHAND GANDHI




Father of the Nation

About the Renowed freedom activist

==>Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi was born on October 2, 1869, in Porbandar, India, into a devout Hindu family.

==>He studied law in London and became a barrister. He later practiced law in South Africa.

==>Gandhi faced racial discrimination in South Africa, which led him to start his first campaigns for civil rights and justice.

==>Gandhi developed the concept of Satyagraha (truth force), advocating nonviolent resistance against injustice and oppression.

==>He returned to India in 1915 and became a prominent leader in the Indian National Congress.

==>Gandhi's leadership in campaigns like the Champaran and Kheda Satyagrahas showcased his dedication to Indian peasants' rights.

==>In 1920, he launched the Non-Cooperation Movement, urging Indians to boycott British institutions and products.The 1930 Salt March was a pivotal event where Gandhi and his followers protested against British salt taxes by making their own salt.

==>As part of the Salt March, Gandhi led a 240-mile journey to Dandi, Gujarat, symbolizing resistance against unjust laws.In 1942, he launched the Quit India Movement, demanding an end to British rule in India.

==>Gandhi was an advocate of religious harmony and worked to bridge gaps between different religious communities.He practiced and preached a simple and frugal lifestyle, promoting self-sufficiency and economic independence.Gandhi was arrested several times for his civil disobedience campaigns and spent a significant amount of time in prison.Tragically, Gandhi was assassinated on January 30, 1948, by a Hindu nationalist who disagreed with his principles.Gandhi's legacy lives on, with his teachings of nonviolence, truth, and compassion continuing to inspire social and political movements worldwide.
